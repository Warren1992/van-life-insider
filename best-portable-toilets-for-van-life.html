Best Portable Toilets for Van Life

A portable toilet may not be the most exciting piece of van life gear, but it can quickly become one of the most appreciated. Whether you're parked in a remote desert campsite, stuck inside during a storm, or simply don't want to search for a public restroom in the middle of the night, having a toilet in your van adds a level of comfort and freedom that's hard to beat.

Image: Van Life Insider

The good news is that van lifers have more options than ever in 2026. From simple bucket systems to premium composting toilets, there is a solution for nearly every budget and travel style.

Cassette toilets are the most common choice

Cassette toilets remain one of the most popular options among van dwellers. They combine a toilet seat and a removable waste tank into a single compact unit.

Many travelers like cassette toilets because they are easy to use, easy to clean, and relatively affordable compared to more advanced systems.

The removable tank can be emptied at RV dump stations or approved disposal facilities, making them practical for long-term travel.

Composting toilets reduce maintenance

Composting toilets separate liquids and solids into different containers. This design helps reduce odors and can significantly extend the time between cleanouts.

Many full-time van lifers prefer composting toilets because they require fewer dump station visits and can feel more like a traditional home bathroom setup.

The downside is cost. Composting toilets are usually one of the most expensive bathroom upgrades in a van build.

Bucket toilets remain surprisingly popular

A basic five-gallon bucket fitted with a toilet seat lid remains one of the cheapest and simplest solutions available.

When paired with waste bags and odor-control products, bucket toilets can work surprisingly well for occasional use.

They are lightweight, inexpensive, and easy to replace if damaged.

Foldable toilets save valuable space

Space is limited in most vans, which is why foldable toilets have gained popularity in recent years.

These compact units can be folded and stored under a bed platform, inside a cabinet, or in a storage compartment when not needed.

For weekend campers and part-time travelers, a foldable toilet may be all that's necessary.

Features worth looking for

  • Leak-resistant waste tank
  • Comfortable seat height
  • Easy cleaning access
  • Compact storage size
  • Odor-control features
  • Durable construction

Before purchasing any toilet, measure your available storage space carefully. Many first-time van builders underestimate how much room a toilet can occupy.

Bottom line

The best portable toilet for van life depends on how often you travel, how much space you have available, and how much maintenance you're willing to perform.

Cassette toilets remain the best all-around option for most van lifers, composting toilets offer premium convenience, bucket systems provide maximum affordability, and foldable toilets work well for occasional adventures.

Whatever option you choose, having a reliable bathroom solution can make life on the road far more comfortable and reduce stress when you're far from traditional facilities.
