How to Get Mail While Living in a Van

One of the first questions people ask when considering van life is surprisingly simple: where does your mail go?

Image: Van Life Insider

Bills, bank cards, driver's license renewals, vehicle registrations, tax documents, and online purchases still need somewhere to be delivered. Fortunately, full-time travelers have several reliable options for receiving mail while living on the road.

Use a family member or trusted friend

Many van lifers use the address of a family member or close friend as their primary mailing address.

Mail can be collected, sorted, and forwarded whenever necessary. This is often the simplest and least expensive option available.

The downside is that you're relying on someone else's schedule and willingness to help long-term.

Mail forwarding services are popular

Mail forwarding companies specialize in helping travelers, RVers, and nomads receive mail while constantly moving.

These services receive your mail, scan envelopes, notify you online, and forward important items to whatever location you're currently visiting.

Many full-time van lifers consider a mail forwarding service one of the most valuable subscriptions they have.

General Delivery can be a lifesaver

The United States Postal Service offers a service called General Delivery.

Instead of delivering mail to a home address, participating post offices will temporarily hold mail for pickup.

This can be useful when you're traveling through a specific town and need important documents delivered while on the road.

Amazon Lockers simplify package delivery

Online shopping becomes much easier when you use Amazon Lockers.

Instead of shipping packages to a residential address, you can have many Amazon orders delivered directly to secure locker locations throughout the country.

Once the package arrives, you'll receive a code that allows you to retrieve it from the locker.

PO Boxes work for part-time travelers

A traditional PO Box can be useful if you spend most of your time in one city or region.

However, for full-time travelers constantly crossing state lines, a PO Box often becomes less convenient than forwarding services or General Delivery.

Don't forget your legal address

Receiving mail and establishing legal residency are not always the same thing.

Vehicle registration, insurance, taxes, voting, and driver's licenses may require a legal domicile address depending on your state.

Before hitting the road full-time, make sure you understand the residency requirements that apply to your situation.

What most van lifers use

  • Family or friend address
  • Mail forwarding service
  • General Delivery
  • Amazon Lockers
  • PO Boxes
  • Package pickup locations

Many experienced travelers actually combine several of these options depending on what they need delivered.

Bottom line

Getting mail while living in a van is easier than most people expect.

Whether you use a trusted family member, a professional mail forwarding service, General Delivery, or Amazon Lockers, there are reliable ways to receive everything from bank cards to online purchases while traveling full-time.

Once your mail system is set up, it quickly becomes just another routine part of life on the road.
