Best States for Full-Time Van Life

Choosing the right state can make a huge difference when you're living in a van full-time. Some states offer endless public land, mild weather, and abundant camping opportunities. Others make van life far more challenging with harsh winters, expensive campgrounds, and limited overnight parking options.

Image: Van Life Insider

No state is perfect for every traveler, but a few consistently rise to the top for affordability, freedom, and year-round adventure.

Arizona remains a van life favorite

Arizona continues to attract thousands of nomads every year thanks to its vast public lands and winter-friendly climate.

Areas around Quartzsite become temporary communities of RVers and van dwellers during the cooler months. Long-term camping areas and abundant Bureau of Land Management land make it easy to find places to stay without spending a fortune.

The biggest challenge is summer heat, which sends many travelers north during the hottest months.

Colorado offers incredible scenery

Colorado is one of the most beautiful states for van life. Mountain towns, national forests, and endless outdoor recreation opportunities make it a dream destination for many travelers.

The downside is that popularity has increased significantly, and finding overnight parking can be more difficult in some areas than it once was.

Winter weather can also be challenging at higher elevations.

Utah provides endless exploration

Utah is packed with public land, stunning desert landscapes, and some of the most famous national parks in the country.

For van lifers who enjoy hiking, photography, and remote camping, few states offer as much variety.

Many travelers spend months exploring Utah's backroads and dispersed camping areas.

New Mexico is often overlooked

New Mexico doesn't always receive the same attention as neighboring states, but it offers many of the same advantages.

Public lands, lower population density, relatively affordable living costs, and a mild climate make it an attractive option for full-time travelers.

It's also a popular place for nomads looking to avoid crowded destinations.

Texas works well for winter travel

Texas is enormous, which means there are countless places to explore.

Many van dwellers appreciate the warmer winter temperatures, extensive road network, and wide variety of landscapes ranging from deserts to coastlines.

However, public land opportunities are more limited compared to western states like Arizona and Utah.

What matters most?

  • Access to public land
  • Mild weather
  • Affordable camping options
  • Reliable internet coverage
  • Access to supplies and services
  • Friendly local regulations

The best state for full-time van life ultimately depends on your priorities. Some travelers chase cool mountain weather, while others follow warm deserts throughout the winter.

Bottom line

Arizona, Colorado, Utah, New Mexico, and Texas remain some of the strongest choices for full-time van life in 2026.

Each offers a different balance of climate, scenery, camping opportunities, and cost of living. The good news is that van life gives you the freedom to move whenever conditions change.

Many successful full-time travelers don't stay in one state at all—they follow the seasons and enjoy the best each region has to offer.
